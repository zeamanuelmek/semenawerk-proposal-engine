# Proposal Template Structure — 17 Sections

This is the master reference for the proposal template used in all SemEnaWerk proposals. Every proposal follows this structure in this exact order with these exact sub-items.

---

## Section 1 — Company Name and Project Owner

**Purpose:** Establish who the company is and the foundational ownership context.

**Required content:**
- Company identification table (kvTable): name, legal form, country, location, sector, project type, project status
- Corporate profile (2-3 paragraphs): what they do, the division structure, how divisions integrate, rationale for the business model
- Project owner and strategic direction (1-2 paragraphs): equity commitment, sponsor conviction, long-term vision

---

## Section 2 — Experience of the Project Promoter

**Purpose:** Establish credibility and de-risk the project for lenders.

**Required content:**
- Sectoral experience overview (what industry background)
- Years of active operational experience (specific to the sector)
- Related sector capabilities (bullet list — minimum 5 bullets)
- Recognition, industry standing, and pre-investment commitments (concrete actions taken)
- Capability implications for the project (how experience de-risks specific project risks)

---

## Section 3 — Project Description

**Purpose:** Give the full picture of what the project is and why it matters.

**Required content:**
- Full project overview (what, where, how much, why — 2-3 paragraphs)
- Division/product breakdown if multi-product (one sub-section per division)
- Integrated operational model and synergies (shared infrastructure, cross-division flows)
- Vision, mission, strategic objectives (bullet list of 5-year objectives)
- Strategic importance and national alignment (alignment with Ethiopian development priorities)

---

## Section 4 — Proposed Project Location

**Purpose:** Justify the site selection and document strategic advantages.

**Required content:**
- Location identification table (kvTable): park name, authority, city, zone, region, country, distances
- Strategic advantage sub-sections (minimum 5, each as an h3):
  - Trade corridor access
  - Industrial infrastructure
  - Market proximity
  - Workforce availability
  - Raw material proximity (if applicable)
  - Logistics/port access
- Regulatory framework and investment incentives (dataTable: incentive, authority, benefit)
- Site configuration paragraph

---

## Section 5 — Product and Process Technology

**Purpose:** Demonstrate technical credibility and describe the production system.

**Required content:**
- Production process description — stage by stage (h3 per stage, minimum 4-6 stages)
- Major machinery list (dataTable: #, machine, origin, qty, cost)
- Equipment selection criteria (bullet list with rationale per origin)
- Product specifications and grades/variants (dataTable: product, specs, volume mix, price range, target customer)
- Quality control and certification roadmap (dataTable: milestone, timeline, authority, revenue impact)

---

## Section 6 — Raw Materials

**Purpose:** Document supply chain, local content %, and sourcing strategy.

**Required content:**
- Raw material overview paragraph mentioning domestic vs imported PERCENTAGES (both by weight and by value)
- Per-division breakdown table (dataTable: material, source, origin %)
- Local % and import % clearly stated
- What specifically is imported (and why it cannot be sourced locally)
- Sourcing strategy: seasonal management, supplier relationships, quality verification, contract structure

---

## Section 7 — Required Land and Shed Specifications

**Purpose:** Document the physical footprint and civil works requirement.

**Required content:**
- Total land area (kvTable: area m², dimensions, tenure, lease cost, configuration)
- Built-up area allocation (dataTable: functional zone, area m², % of built-up)
- Outdoor area allocation (dataTable: zone, area m², purpose)
- Construction and civil works description (materials, standards, food-grade specs if applicable)
- Site layout and material flow logic

---

## Section 8 — Planned Initial Investment Capital and Capital Structure

**Purpose:** Present the full investment picture with transparent funding structure.

**Required content:**
- Total CAPEX (kvTable: ETB total, USD equivalent, funding structure summary)
- Capital structure table (dataTable: source, ETB amount, proportion %)
- Detailed CAPEX breakdown (dataTable: component, ETB, %)
- Investment allocation analysis (why the allocation makes sense)
- Investment timing / cash draw-down (phased disbursement across implementation)

---

## Section 9 — Financial Feasibility

**Purpose:** Prove the project is bankable with full financial analysis.

**Required content:**
- Key financial assumptions (kvTable: exchange rate, inflation, loan rate, tenor, grace, tax, depreciation)
- Revenue forecast 5-year (dataTable by division if applicable, with growth %)
- Operating cost forecast (dataTable with cost ratio)
- Income statement 5-10 year (EBITDA, depreciation, EBIT, interest, PBT, tax, net profit)
- Cash flow projections (operating CF, debt service, net CF, cumulative)
- Key financial indicators (dataTable: indicator, value, benchmark, rating)
- Break-even analysis (fixed costs, variable cost/tonne, contribution margin, break-even volume, break-even % of capacity)
- Sensitivity analysis (dataTable: scenario, key metric, status — minimum 5 scenarios)

---

## Section 10 — Production and Service Capacity

**Purpose:** Document operating schedule and capacity trajectory with explicit calculations.

**Required content:**
- Operating schedule (kvTable): MUST include the explicit calculation:
  - Working hours per shift
  - Shifts per day
  - Working days per week
  - Working days per year
  - Annual hours = shifts × hours × days
- Annual production capacity at full operation (dataTable per product/division)
- Capacity utilization trajectory Y1→Y5 (dataTable showing % per division per year)
- Projected production output in actual tonnes per year
- Capacity expansion potential

---

## Section 11 — Market Destination

**Purpose:** Prove there is a market and document both domestic and export strategy with numbers.

**Required content:**
- Market strategy overview (state the domestic/export % split explicitly)
- Export destination table (dataTable: country, product focus, market rationale)
- Export QUANTITY projections at maturity (tonnes per destination)
- Export SALES VALUE at maturity (ETB millions)
- Domestic quantity table
- Domestic sales value table
- Target customer segments (bullet list per division)
- Pricing strategy table (grade/product, price by year)
- Competitive landscape table

---

## Section 12 — Utility and Infrastructure

**Purpose:** Document all operational utility requirements with specifics.

**MANDATORY sub-sections (each as h2):**
1. **Electrical Power** — kvTable: base load kW, peak kW, connected load, source, backup, annual consumption, annual cost, expansion provision
2. **Water Supply** — kvTable: industrial demand m³/day, domestic demand, total, source, storage capacity, wastewater management
3. **Telecommunications** — kvTable: voice, internet, internal network, ERP bandwidth, security
4. **HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)** — kvTable: production area, specialized areas (food-grade, paint booth, carding), admin, maintenance schedule
5. **Road Infrastructure** — kvTable: access road, internal roads, loading bays type and number, truck turning radius, surface specification
6. Supporting infrastructure paragraph (security, waste management, fire safety)

---

## Section 13 — Implementation Schedule

**Purpose:** Show a realistic, credible path to commercial operation.

**Required content:**
- Overview table (kvTable: commencement, completion, launch, total months)
- Phased schedule (dataTable: phase, key activities, timeline, milestone) — minimum 6 phases
- Critical path and risk management paragraph
- Gantt visualization table (simplified, showing months across the top)

---

## Section 14 — Socio-Economic Benefit

**Purpose:** Document the full social and economic value beyond commercial returns. This section is often under-written — give it serious depth.

**MANDATORY sub-sections:**
- Economic Contribution (GDP growth, value addition, GVC integration)
- Export Potential and Hard Currency Generation (USD figure mandatory)
- Contribution to Import Substitution (list specific import categories substituted)
- Skill and Knowledge Transfer and Spillover Effect
- Enhanced Tax Revenue — MUST SPLIT:
  - **14.x.1 Direct Tax Revenue** (CIT, payroll tax — calculate ETB amount)
  - **14.x.2 Indirect Tax Revenue** (VAT, customs, excise)
- Social Benefit and Employment — MUST SPLIT:
  - **14.x.1 Permanent Employment** (dataTable: role, Y1 headcount, Y3 headcount, female %, monthly ETB)
  - **14.x.2 Temporary Employment** (construction phase)
  - **14.x.3 Indirect Employment** (supply chain, distribution, dealers)
- Improved Quality of Life and Access to Basic Services
- Income Redistribution Effect
- Corporate Social Responsibility
- Education and Skills Training Programs
- Health and Safety Standards
- Summary Table (dataTable: benefit category, key impact — covers all sub-sections)

---

## Section 15 — Environmental Impact

**Purpose:** Address all environmental considerations with specific mitigation measures.

**Required content:**
- Environmental framework overview (EPA category, why this classification)
- Potential negative impacts — COVER EACH AS h3:
  - Impact on Air Quality
  - Impact on Water Quality
  - Impact on Soil
  - Impact on Fauna and Flora
  - Impact on Human Health
  - Other Environmental Considerations (energy, noise, waste)
- Mitigation measures matrix (dataTable: impact area, mitigation measures)
- Environmental management system paragraph
- Sustainable operations and resource efficiency
- Compliance and regulatory framework

---

## Section 16 — Conclusion

**Purpose:** Summarize the case and make specific recommendations.

**Required content:**
- Strategic project merits
- Financial viability justification (cite IRR, DSCR, payback, NPV by name)
- Environmental sustainability justification
- Socio-economic sustainability justification
- Overall justification and recommendation paragraph
- Specific recommendations — split by stakeholder:
  - To the financing institution (DBE/bank) — bullet list
  - To the promoter — bullet list

---

## Section 17 — Promoter's Contact Address

**Purpose:** Provide contact information and document control.

**Required content:**
- Promoter contact details kvTable — use [placeholder] fields for client to fill in:
  - Company name, authorized representative, position, phone (primary), phone (secondary), email, physical address, postal address
- Document control kvTable: title, version, prepared by, date, exchange rate, tax rate, classification, distribution
- Engagement welcome paragraph
- Final line: *End of [Company Name] Project Proposal.* — centered, italic

---

## Formatting Rules

| Element | Style |
|---|---|
| Section number | Arabic (1, 2, 3...) |
| Sub-section number | Decimal (1.1, 1.2, 2.1...) |
| Sub-sub-section | Triple decimal (14.6.1, 14.7.3...) |
| Table caption | Bold paragraph above the table, ending in colon |
| Page breaks | After every major section (before next h1) |
| Paragraphs | Justified, 1.5 line spacing, 160 spacing after |
| Cover page | Company name centered, navy 26pt bold; subtitle centered navy 16pt; slogan bottom gold border |
